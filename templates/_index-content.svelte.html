Looking back at Minnesota's past presidential tickets

By Jeff Hargarten • Star Tribune

February 6, 2019

With Sen. Amy Klobuchar announcing her entry into a crowded 2020 Democratic presidential field this week, she joins the ranks of a other Minnesotans who have competed in America's biggest political contest.

Past presidential candidates from Minnesota haven't fared very well, with only one ever emerging from the primaries with the nomination. While Minnesota historically boasts two Vice Presidents, its record at helping get its own into the Oval Office is a less impressive 0-5.

Klobuchar is a wildly popular senator in Minnesota, as evidenced by her crushing election victories, including 2018's 60 percent vote share. How her local popularity translates to the national stage remains to be seen.

Here's how the past presidential candidacies of Minnesota's politicians have performed throughout time:

Eugene McCarthy

1968 Democratic Primary

Having represented Minnesota in both the U.S. House of Representatives and the U.S. Senate, Eugene McCarthy also ran for president five different times. McCarthy's most famous campaign was his attempt to primary President Lyndon B. Johnson in 1968 on an anti-Vietnam War platform.

The primary was thrown into chaos when Johnson ultimately declined to seek a second term, pitting McCarthy against fellow Minnesotan and U.S. Vice President, Hubert Humphrey, who won the nomination, despite McCarthy faring well in prior state contests.

McCarthy ran for president again in 1972 and 1992 as a Democrat, and as an independent in 1976 and 1998. He didn't get very far any of those races.

Hubert H. Humphrey

1968 General Election

Despite not campaigning in the messy 1968 primary, former U.S. Senator from Minnesota and Vice President of the United States Hubert Humphrey was nominated for president by the Democratic Party.

Minnesota has famously been one of the most reliably Democratic states in the union during presidential election years, with the state only voting for Republicans three times in its whole history. This election was the most recent of those times, when Humphrey couldn't carry his home state and Richard Nixon won the presidency in a landslide.

Walter Mondale

1984 General Election

After a long history in national politics, which included serving as Minnesota's attorney general, U.S. Senator, and ultimately Jimmy Carter's vice president, Walter Mondale won the Democratic nomination for president in 1984.

Minnesota stuck with its long history of Democratic voting and was the only state (plus Washington D.C.) to vote for Mondale, who suffered a crushing loss in Ronald Reagan's reelection landslide.

Tim Pawlenty and Michelle Bachmann

2012 Republican Primary

Former Minnesota Gov. Tim Pawlenty declared his candidacy for the GOP presidential nomination in 2011, but dropped out less than three months later after finishing third in the Ames Straw Poll.

Former Minnesota Rep. Michelle Bachmann won that same straw poll with a quarter of the votes. Bachmann then came in sixth place in the Iowa caucuses, prompting her to leave the race, though she remained on most ballots until Super Tuesday.

Both Bachmann and Pawlenty eventually endorsed Mitt Romney for the Republican nomination, which he won. Romney, of course, lost to President Barack Obama who went on to a second term.
